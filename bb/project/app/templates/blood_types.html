ALL BLOOD TYPES:

There are eight major blood types, each determined by the presence or absence of specific antigens on the surface of red blood cells. These blood types are created from two main blood groups:

  • A+: Has the A antigen and Rh factor (+)
  • A-: Has the A antigen but lacks the Rh factor (-)
  • B+: Contains the B antigen and Rh factor (+)
  • B-: Carries the B antigen but lacks the Rh factor (-)
  • AB+: Has both A and B antigens as well as the Rh factor (+)
  • AB-: Contains both A and B antigens but lacks the Rh factor (-)
  • O+: Lacks A and B antigens but has the Rh factor (+)
  • O-: Lacks A, B antigens, and the Rh factor (-)
